Who We Are

We are a team of Civil Engineering researchers from Imperial College London.

We need data to compile comprehensive studies of the water supply networks, and to understand why some people still don't have clean, reliable, safe water coming out of their faucet every hour of every day - despite having piped water supply to their homes.

We realised that there wasn't enough data available for our research to have an impact on the world, so we started the Networking Water initiative.

Networking Water aims to promote a community initiative to monitor the quality and quantity of the water and electricity supply in developing countries, where there is a lack of data available on water distribution.

We believe that citizens are the key to bridging this data gap. Become a citizen scientist and help us collect data on your water & electricty supply, to make a measurable difference in data science!
